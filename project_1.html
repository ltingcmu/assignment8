Currently, 40% of recycled plastic is not done so properly, and 62% of people worry that lack of knowledge causes incorrect recycling.

Critical environmental issues are causing companies and consumers to rethink their relationships with plastic packaging. I teamed up with a designer, industrial engineer, and business analysts all from Carnegie Mellon, in order to tackle this problem posed to us by Unilever for Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Business Design Challenge 2021.

Here is how we arrived at our solution of a "Recycle & Reward" program that reimagined the consumer experience with Vaseline, starting with the physical lotion bottle packaging, and ending with gamifying recycling.

Physical Design Solution

We 3D printed a packaging prototype for Vaseline to address user concerns.

We replaced the traditional pump with an airless pump to decrease the amount of residue lotion wasted, addressing the key recycling user pain point.

We maintained a tall, slender bottle, with the cap & bottom in the shape of Vaseline's classic logo.


“This solution feels luxurious because I don’t have to get reach in and scoop out cream, which is gross, and I also don’t need to worry about wasting lotion or having it crust on the top like with a typical pump."

- Anisa J., User Testing

Service Design Solution

Pay It Forward Program Overview

Nir Eyal's ‘Hooked Model’

We drew from a powerful, research-baked framework to get consumers excited about recycling, creating a closed loop bottle reuse system. Our goal was to save manufacturing costs and drive new consumer acquisition & brand loyalty through an in-store kiosk experience.

Yu-Kai Chow’s Actionable Gamification Model

We also wanted users to be constantly delighted by variable rewards, to focus on nonprofit causes and sustainability evangelization in a way that doesn’t feel like pushing a company’s agenda, and feel a part of a local community that’s making real environmental impact.


Trigger

Buying or Finishing Bottle

User finishes their bottle of Vaseline lotion and scans the QR code on the back of the bottle for information on how to recycle.


Action

Goes to Store

User learns about the Vaseline pay-it-forward recycling program and finds their nearest drop-off location.


Reward

A Surprise Each Time

The user follows the steps on the recycling kiosk to recycle the bottle and receive a variable reward.

Each lotion bottle will have a unique, 9-digit code on the inside of the cap.
