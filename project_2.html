User Research

Social media users come across ads and posts everyday as they scroll through news feeds. Occasionally users report ads. But what happens after they report, and how do they feel? What will make them stay vigilant?

Research Methods

User Interviews

Contextual Inquiry

Speed Dating

Semi-structured interviews were conducted to understand user's overall feelings and experiences with reporting ads in their feeds.

Directed storytelling and task analysis helped garner insights into user behavior.

We gathered feedback on 12 early concept storyboard cards relating to reporting ads.

Synthesis

Initial Hypotheses

After synthesizing our findings from initial interviews, data analysis, and background research we came up with several hypotheses related to how users interact with and experience algorithms and ads.

  • Transparency of ad targeting is poor
  • Reporting & Ad settings are poorly designed, with too many steps and opaque phrases
  • Users aren't highly motivated to report ads unless they feel strong emotions like anger
  • Regulation is underutilized and under-explored
